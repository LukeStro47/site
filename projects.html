Center for Psychedelic Policy
Policy Researcher
July 2025 — Present

Making the fiscal and legislative case for expanded access to psilocybin-assisted therapy in the United States, with a focus on Oregon's 2027 Medicaid session. Alongside research, developing email campaigns and fundraising strategy.

Projects
Why

Psilocybin-assisted therapy is legal in Oregon, but access is effectively limited to those who can pay out of pocket. The 2027 legislative session is the window to change that — and the argument needs to be fiscal and legislative, not just moral.

How

Co-authoring a policy report synthesizing cost-effectiveness data from Oregon Psilocybin Services, mapping the state's behavioral health landscape, and constructing the case for integrating PAT within existing Medicaid structures. Alongside research, developing CPP's email campaigns and contributing to fundraising strategy — translating technical work into communications that reach donors and decision-makers.

Queen's University Belfast
Research Assistant — Critical Civility Project
April 2024 — December 2024

Qualitative research on the civic outcomes of Philosophy for Children programs, as part of Dr. Suzanne Whitten's Critical Civility Project. Thematic analysis of 200+ student surveys, co-authored publication, and conference organisation.

Projects
Why

Dr. Suzanne Whitten's project asked whether teaching philosophy to children could improve democratic thinking and civic behaviour — a question with real downstream stakes for education policy and for what philosophy is actually for.

How

Conducted a thematic analysis of 200+ primary school student surveys from a Philosophy for Children pilot program, assessing impact on civility and democratic engagement. Co-authored a paper with Dr. Whitten that was accepted by the Journal of Philosophy in Schools. Helped plan a conference in Belfast bringing together leading Philosophy for Children advocates.
